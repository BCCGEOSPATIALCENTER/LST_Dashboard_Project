How Land Surface Temperature is Calculated — Step by Step

STEP 01
Satellite Capture
Landsat 8/9 flies at 705 km altitude. Its thermal infrared sensor (Band 10, ~10.9 µm wavelength) records radiance from every 30×30 m patch of ground.
STEP 02
DN → Radiance
Raw digital numbers are converted to top-of-atmosphere spectral radiance using USGS calibration coefficients stored in the image metadata.
L = ML × DN + AL
STEP 03
Radiance → Temperature
USGS Collection 2 Level-2 applies the Planck function inversion to convert radiance to Brightness Temperature in Kelvin, then to Celsius.
LST (°C) = 0.00341802 × DN + 149.0 − 273.15
STEP 04
Artifact Removal
Cloud edges, scan-line errors, and sensor fill values produce unrealistic pixels. We remove them using a 2-pass statistical mask: finite values only, then lower bound = mean − 3×std.
keep: mean − 3σ ≤ pixel ≤ 90°C
STEP 05
UHI Calculation
UHI intensity = P95 minus Mean. Using the 95th percentile instead of raw max avoids single-pixel outliers skewing the result. Represents the hottest 5% vs city average.
UHI = P95 − Mean (°C)
All Metrics Explained
Mean LST
avg(all valid pixels) °C
Average land surface temperature across the entire city area. Represents a typical mix of roads, buildings, parks, and open land. Used as the baseline for UHI calculation.
95th Percentile LST (P95)
percentile(pixels, 95) °C
The temperature that 95% of all pixels fall below. Represents the hottest neighbourhood of the city — rooftops, asphalt, industrial zones — without being skewed by single outlier pixels.
UHI Intensity
P95 − Mean (°C)
The gap between the hottest 5% of surfaces and the city average. A larger gap means stronger, more concentrated heat island effect. This is the primary indicator used for risk classification.
Risk Level
LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL
Calculated in Python from UHI intensity: LOW <15°C, MEDIUM 15–25°C, HIGH 25–35°C, CRITICAL >35°C. The AI writes the narrative but Python controls the risk label — ensuring consistency.
Min LST
min(valid pixels) °C — after 2-pass mask
Lowest valid surface temperature in the city area. After artifact removal (mean − 3σ lower bound), this represents shaded vegetation, water bodies, or cool rural fringes.
Max LST
max(valid pixels) °C — QA reference only
Highest single pixel temperature. Retained for quality assurance and data integrity checking. Not used in UHI calculation because one anomalous pixel (metal rooftop, bare rock) can be unrepresentative.
Valid Pixels
count(mask = TRUE)
Number of 30×30 m pixels used in the analysis after removing cloud, shadow, nodata, and statistical artifact pixels. Each pixel covers 900 m² of ground surface.
Std Dev (σ)
std(all valid pixels) °C
Standard deviation of LST across the city. High std dev means large variation between hot and cool surfaces (strong spatial contrast). Low std dev means temperatures are more uniform.
